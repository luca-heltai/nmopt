First-Order Admissible Directions¶

Linearized admissibility at uˉ\bar uuˉ:

∇φj(uˉ)⋅d≥0∀j∈A(uˉ).\nabla\varphi_j(\bar u)\cdot d\ge 0\quad \forall j\in\mathcal A(\bar u).∇φj​(uˉ)⋅d≥0∀j∈A(uˉ).

Interpretation:

  • negative product violates constraint jjj at first order

  • nonnegative product keeps constraint jjj admissible at first order

First-Order Necessary Condition¶

At a local constrained minimizer:

∇f(uˉ)⋅d≥0for all admissible d.\nabla f(\bar u)\cdot d\ge 0\quad\text{for all admissible }d.∇f(uˉ)⋅d≥0for all admissible d.
(5)

Why:

  • if an admissible ddd had ∇f(uˉ)⋅d<0\nabla f(\bar u)\cdot d<0∇f(uˉ)⋅d<0

  • then uˉ+td\bar u+t duˉ+td would decrease fff for small t>0t>0t>0

  • contradiction with local optimality

Summary¶

  1. No admissible descent directions at a constrained minimum.

  2. This gives gradient balance with active constraint normals.

  3. That balance is KKT with nonnegative multipliers.

  4. Second order checks Lagrangian curvature on critical directions.
